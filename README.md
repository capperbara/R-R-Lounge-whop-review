### Sports Betting Legality and Compliance

  

It's important to understand the legal landscape before joining any sports betting community.

  

In the United States, sports betting was previously prohibited under the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA). However, the Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018, opening the door for individual states to legalize sports betting.

  

As of January 2024, 37 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico have operational legalized sports betting. Additionally, 30 states plus D.C. and Puerto Rico allow online sports betting, though some states restrict betting to physical locations only.

  

Since the 2018 Supreme Court decision, over $20 billion has been wagered on sports betting in the United States, demonstrating the massive growth of this industry.

  

**Important:** Always verify that sports betting is legal in your jurisdiction before placing any wagers. R&R Lounge provides picks and analysis, but it's your responsibility to ensure compliance with local laws.
